Stars

What are stars?

Stars are huge balls of hot, glowing gas (mostly hydrogen and helium) that produce light and heat through nuclear fusion.

Life Cycle of A Star:

  1. Nebula – stars form inside giant clouds of gas and dust.
  2. Protostar – the gas condenses and heats up.
  3. Main Sequence Star – stable phase (like our Sun).
  4. Red Giant / Supergiant – star expands when fuel runs out.
  5. End stage – Small/medium stars → White Dwarf → Black Dwarf
    Massive stars → Supernova → Neutron Star or Black Hole

Types of Stars

  1. Red Dwarfs – small, cool, and long-living (most common).
  2. Yellow Stars – like our Sun.
  3. Blue Giants – very hot, bright, and short-lived.
  4. Neutron Stars – incredibly dense remains of supernova explosions.

Fun Facts

  • The Sun is about 4.6 billion years old and halfway through its life.
  • The largest stars can be 1,000 times bigger than the Sun.

Galaxies

What are galaxies?

Galaxies are massive systems of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter bound together by gravity. Each galaxy can contain millions to trillions of stars.

Types of Galaxies

  • Spiral galaxies – like our Milky Way, with a bright center and spiral arms.
  • Elliptical galaxies – round or oval, filled with older stars.
  • Irregular galaxies – no clear shape, often formed by collisions.
  • Lenticular galaxies – disk-shaped with a central bulge, but no spiral arms, containing mostly older stars and little new star formation.

Milky Way Galaxy

The Milky Way Galaxy is a vast spiral system about 100,000 light-years wide, containing hundreds of billions of stars, planets, gas, and dust. Our solar system sits in the Orion Arm, halfway between the center and the edge. At its core lies Sagittarius A*, a supermassive black hole. The spiral arms hold star-forming regions and glowing nebulae, while the outer halo has older stars and clusters. From Earth, the Milky Way appears as a hazy band of light across the night sky. In about 4.5 billion years, it will merge with the Andromeda Galaxy, creating a larger system.

Fun Facts

  • The Andromeda Galaxy is our nearest large neighbor, about 2.5 million light-years away.
  • Galaxies often collide — in about 4.5 billion years, the Milky Way will merge with Andromeda.
